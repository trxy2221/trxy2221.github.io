Essay

We live in a busy world. While most of the time we seem to be able to cope with a fast pace and seemingly endless jobs, we might not see that our pressure is piling up along the way. When we can sense the pressure, it might have already grown too much as many different things have contributed to the problem. The secret to happiness is thus to understand why we are unhappy, which is, surprisingly, a difficult task.

When our pressure increases, many of us could choose to avoid directly dealing with the pressure, because it is easier to distract yourself from the pressure by, for example, having some fun. However, when the fun comes to an end, the pressure and the associated negative emotion would hit you again very quickly - it is never successful to try to forget your pressure. To make your life happy again, we really have to get to the bottom of your problem to truly solve it.

Figuring out the underlying "causes" of the problem is, ironically, the most challenging part. Note that we are talking about "causes", not just "a cause". We might be able to identify some potential factors such as the unfinished works of your job, your arguments with your significant other, loud noises surrounding your place, or just some sleep issue. But we need to know which ones truly bother us. Maybe we have not heard of loud noises more recently, or we have already fixed the relationship with out significant other. Gradually, we can narrow down to real culprits.

If we really don’t know where to start, here’s a quick tip – begin with your procrastinated works. They are usually among the common attributes of your rising pressure. When we are busy or exhausted, we are inclined to delay less urgent works, and maybe you have already had a very long to-do list, if you think about it. The impact of taking care of your delayed works is obvious, because you know there is one less thing to deal with, so you would feel less pressure. In addition, the act also gives you a sense of achievement, which helps ease your pressure, too.

It is very frequent that we wait too long for our pressure to take away our happy life. Part of the reason is the avoidance and difficulty of analyzing the source of the pressure. It takes effort and time to realize what causes our pressure, and many of us choose not to do it because it is not a pleasant process. However, one must go through the reflection and self-examination stage to live a genuinely happy life.
